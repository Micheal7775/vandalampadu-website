History of Vandalampadu

Vandalampadu village traces its roots to an ancient settlement known as Palaya Oor (Pallayur). This earlier settlement existed several centuries ago and was home to a small but close-knit community. Over time, Palaya Oor faced severe natural and environmental challenges. Frequent illnesses, lack of clean water, and unsafe living conditions made daily life difficult for the residents. Houses were often damaged due to natural causes, and people struggled to maintain a healthy livelihood. Due to these hardships, many families decided to migrate peacefully in search of a safer and healthier place to live. Some families settled in the nearby area that later came to be known as Valan Nagar, which is now present-day Vandalampadu. Other families migrated to surrounding villages such as Kallikulam, Mullaikadu, and nearby regions. Even today, the history of Palaya Oor is preserved through oral traditions passed down from generation to generation. A few families still safeguard old belongings and artifacts that serve as living evidence of the earlier settlement.

St. Joseph’s Church

St. Joseph’s Church has always been the backbone of Vandalampadu village. In the early days, before a formal church was built, villagers gathered in homes to pray and worship together. These prayer meetings strengthened unity, trust, and spiritual discipline among the people. The people of Vandalampadu firmly believe that St. Joseph has always been their protector and guide. The annual church festival is celebrated with great devotion from August 26 to September 4.

Vandalampadu Today

Today, Vandalampadu stands as a well-settled and peaceful village where families live with stability, dignity, and mutual respect. A majority of the working population is employed in various professional and industrial fields, both within India and abroad. Education, discipline, and mutual support continue to guide Vandalampadu toward sustainable growth, while faith and cultural values remain firmly preserved across generations.

About Vandalampadu

Vandalampadu is a small village (hamlet) located in Radhapuram Block, Tirunelveli District of Tamil Nadu, India. It comes under T. Kallikulam Panchayath. The village is situated about 45 KM towards the south of the district headquarters Tirunelveli, 16 KM from Radhapuram, and approximately 693 KM from the state capital Chennai. Vandalampadu’s PIN code is 627117, and the postal head office is Vallioor. Nearby villages include Achampadu (3 KM), Kumbikulam (6 KM), Kannanallur (7 KM), Kovankulam (8 KM), and Dhalapathisamudram (9 KM). Vandalampadu is surrounded by Radhapuram Block to the east, Valliyoor Block to the south, Kalakadu Block to the north, and Thovala Block to the west. The nearest cities to Vandalampadu are Vadakkuvalliyur, Panagudi, Nagercoil, and Tirunelveli. As the village is located near the Bay of Bengal, the climate is generally humid, with warm weather conditions prevailing for most of the year.
